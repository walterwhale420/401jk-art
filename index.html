The raffle FAQ

Everything you need to know

No trust required — the draw is on-chain and anyone can verify it.

Qualifying & Entries

How do I qualify for the raffle?

Just hold a 401jK NFT. That's it. No sign-ups, no claiming, no staking. Every NFT is entered automatically, every month.

Can I hold several NFTs in the same wallet?

Yes. Each NFT counts as one entry. Hold three NFTs, you get three shots at the monthly 15,000 401jK token prize.

The Draw

How is the winner determined?

At 12:00 PM UTC on the first Sunday of each month, we take a snapshot of the Solana blockchain. The first block hash after that snapshot picks the winner - we convert it to a number and run a simple modulo calculation to land on an NFT. The mod team confirms which NFT won and which wallet held it, then sends the prize straight from the community wallet.

How exactly do you calculate the winner?

We take the first Solana block hash after 12:00 PM UTC (first Sunday of each month) and convert it to a number (hex). Divide that number by the total number of NFTs and take the remainder (0 to N-1), add 1, and that's your winning NFT (1 to N). If the collection grows, we divide by the new total.

How can I verify the draw is fair?

Every step is on-chain and public. You can check the snapshot block, the hash, and the calculation yourself. You don't have to take our word for it.

Can the same NFT win several times?

Yes. Previous winners stay in the pool. It's random every time - no cooldown, no rotation. The same NFT could win twice in a row.

Timing & Edge Cases

What if I sell my NFT after the winner is drawn?

You keep the prize. The wallet holding the NFT at snapshot time gets paid - not whoever holds it later. Win on Sunday, sell on Monday, the tokens still come to you.

What if I buy an NFT just after it has won?

You missed that month's prize, but you're in for every draw after. Past wins don't count against you.

Winning

What do I have to do if my NFT wins?

Nothing. The prize is sent directly to the wallet that held the winning NFT at snapshot time. No claims, no forms.

What should I do when I win?

Be happy. Post about it on X and tag @401_JK. And hold your bag.

The Program

For how long will the monthly raffle continue?

The first 12 months are locked in and fully funded. After that, we look at the community wallet and decide. We want to keep it running indefinitely, but we won't make promises we can't back up.

Who gets the money from NFT sales and transaction fees?

All of it goes into the community wallet, which funds the raffles. The artists gave their time for free. Nobody takes a cut.

The Launch

How does the Dutch Auction work?

The auction runs for one hour on June 7, from 3:00 to 4:00 PM UTC. Every NFT starts at 4.01 SOL. The price drops every ten minutes until it hits the floor at 0.401 SOL. You decide what you're willing to pay — when the price reaches that point, you buy. If someone else gets there first, it's gone.

What is the price schedule?

3:00 PM — 4.01 SOL · 3:10 PM — 2.0 SOL · 3:20 PM — 1.5 SOL · 3:30 PM — 1.0 SOL · 3:40 PM — 0.8 SOL · 3:50 PM — 0.6 SOL · 4:00 PM — 0.401 SOL (floor). All times UTC.

What happens to NFTs that don't sell during the auction?

Unsold NFTs stay listed at the floor price of 0.401 SOL for 48 hours. After that, the community wallet sweeps what's left and they're given away to active community members.

Where does the money from the sale go?

All proceeds go into the community wallet. No team cut, no creator fees. Everything funds the monthly raffle prizes for NFT holders.
